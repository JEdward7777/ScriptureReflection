| Reference | Verse | Translation Notes |
|:---------:|-------|-------------------|
|EPH 3:1|For this reason, I, Paul, a prisoner of Christ Jesus on behalf of you Gentiles,|In this verse, Paul identifies himself as a prisoner (or 'bound') for the sake of Christ, emphasizing his commitment and suffering for the sake of the Gentiles. This reflects the themes of suffering for the gospel and God's purpose for the nations, which are prevalent throughout Paul's writings.|
|EPH 3:2|Surely you have heard of the stewardship of God’s grace that was given to me for you,|In this verse, Paul refers to the administration ('οἰκονομίαν') of God's grace, indicating a responsible stewardship given to him specifically for the benefit of the Gentiles. The concept of grace as an entrusted gift is significant throughout Paul's letters, where he emphasizes its importance in the believer's life and ministry.|
|EPH 3:3|That by revelation, He made known to me the mystery, as I have briefly written to you before.|In this translation, the term 'revelation' is used to convey the idea of divine disclosure of the mystery. The phrase 'as I briefly wrote before' is intended to suggest that the mystery has been previously hinted at, aligning with the idea of progressive revelation found throughout scripture.|
|EPH 3:4|When you read this, you will be able to perceive my insight into the mystery of Christ.|The phrase 'that you may be able to understand my insight' emphasizes the apostle's intent for the readers to grasp the depth of the mystery concerning Christ. The term 'mystery' denotes a divine truth not accessible to human reasoning but revealed by God.|
|EPH 3:5|This mystery was not made known to previous generations of mankind as it has now been revealed to His holy apostles and prophets through the Spirit.|This verse highlights the unique revelation of God’s mystery, previously hidden, which has now been unveiled to the apostles and prophets by the Spirit. The focus is on the continuity and unfolding of God's revelation throughout history, emphasizing that this understanding is a gift granted to the holy ones, contrasting it with the ignorance of prior generations.|
|EPH 3:6|This is that the Gentiles are to be fellow heirs, one body, and partakers of His promise in Christ through the gospel.|The phrase 'fellow heirs' is translated to reflect the idea of shared inheritance among the nations and emphasizes unity in Christ. 'Body' indicates being a part of the church, while 'partakers' highlights the active role of Gentiles in God's promise.|
|EPH 3:7|Of this gospel, I became a minister by the gift of God's grace, which was given to me according to the working of His power.|This verse emphasizes the role of Paul as a servant (or minister) in relation to God's grace, highlighting the gift given to him and the divine power at work within him.|
|EPH 3:8|To me, the very least of all the saints, this grace was given, to preach to the Gentiles the unsearchable riches of Christ.|In this verse, Paul emphasizes his position as the least among the saints and the grace bestowed upon him to proclaim the gospel to the Gentiles. The phrase 'the unsearchable riches of Christ' highlights the depth and abundance of Christ's blessings that are difficult to fully understand or explore. This translation aims to capture the humility of Paul and the divine grace he experienced.|
|EPH 3:9|and to enlighten everyone about the stewardship of the mystery that has been hidden from the ages in God, who created all things through Jesus Christ.|This verse emphasizes the act of God revealing the mystery of His plan to all people, which was previously hidden from ages past. The terms 'oikonomia' (stewardship or management) and 'mystery' are significant, highlighting God's sovereign plan for salvation and its revelation through Christ.|
|EPH 3:10|so that the manifold wisdom of God might now be made known to the rulers and authorities in the heavenly places through the church.|This verse highlights the multifaceted wisdom of God as revealed through the church, signifying that the divine plan is made known to celestial beings through earthly institutions. The term 'principalities and powers' reflects both spiritual authorities and cosmic order.|
|EPH 3:11|This was in accordance with the eternal purpose that He accomplished in Christ Jesus our Lord.|In this verse, the phrase 'according to the purpose of the ages' emphasizes the eternal plan of God that is fulfilled in Christ Jesus. The reference to 'Christ Jesus our Lord' anchors the significance of this purpose in the life and mission of Jesus, underscoring the centrality of Christ in God’s redemptive work.|
|EPH 3:12|In Him, we have boldness and confident access through faith in Him.|The terms 'confidence' and 'access' emphasize the believer's ability to approach God without fear. 'Through faith in Him' indicates that this boldness is rooted in faith in Christ, not in our own merit.|
|EPH 3:13|Therefore, I ask you not to lose heart because of my sufferings on your behalf, which are your glory.|This verse conveys Paul’s appeal to the Ephesians not to lose heart due to his sufferings for their sake. The Greek word 'ἐκκακεῖν' implies a sense of becoming discouraged or losing heart. The translation emphasizes the duality of trials and the glory that comes to the believers.|
|EPH 3:14|For this reason, I bow my knees before the Father of our Lord Jesus Christ,|The Greek phrase 'κάμπτω τὰ γόνατά μου' is rendered as 'I bow my knees', emphasizing the act of humility and reverence. The phrase 'πρὸς τὸν πατέρα' conveys the directness of prayer towards God, our Father, which is a central theme in Christian worship.|
|EPH 3:15|from whom every family in heaven and on earth derives its name,|The term 'patriὰ' refers to family or lineage, emphasizing the collective identity of believers as a unified family under God. The phrase indicates that every family in heaven and on earth is named after the Father, which reflects a universal recognition of God's authority and familial relationship with humanity.|
|EPH 3:16|that he may grant you, according to the riches of his glory, to be strengthened with might by his Spirit in the inner man,|In this translation, I have emphasized the notion of divine strength and the inner man. The phrase 'according to the riches of his glory' reflects the abundance of God's resources. 'Strengthened' conveys the action of the Holy Spirit in empowering the believer internally.|
|EPH 3:17|so that Christ may dwell in your hearts through faith; and that you, being rooted and grounded in love,|In this verse, 'dwell' or 'to make one's home' implies a deep, abiding presence. The phrase 'through faith' emphasizes the believer's trust and reliance on Christ, linking faith to the inner transformation of the heart. The term 'heart' is understood here as the center of emotional and spiritual life.|
|EPH 3:18|may be empowered to comprehend, along with all the saints, the vast dimensions of God's love—its width, length, depth, and height.|This verse emphasizes the breadth, length, depth, and height of God's love, encouraging believers to grasp the magnitude of this love together with all saints.|
|EPH 3:19|and to know the love of Christ that surpasses knowledge, that you may be filled with all the fullness of God.|The phrase 'the surpassing love of Christ' reflects the richness of understanding the nature of Christ's love, which transcends mere knowledge and invites a deeper relational experience. This translation emphasizes not only comprehension but also fullness, tying into the overarching themes of divine completeness found throughout Ephesians.|
|EPH 3:20|Now to Him who is able to do far more abundantly than all that we ask or think, according to the power that is at work within us,|This verse highlights God's ability to accomplish far beyond our requests or thoughts, emphasizing His limitless power that works within believers. The phrase 'according to the power at work within us' suggests an active, present reality of God's power operating in the lives of Christians, which serves as the foundation for the boundless possibilities in prayer and faith.|
|EPH 3:21|To Him be glory in the Church and in Christ Jesus throughout all generations, forever and ever. Amen.|In this translation, I aimed to maintain the reverent tone appropriate to the context of worship and praise, reflecting the eternal aspect of God's glory as well as its manifestation through the Church and in Christ Jesus. The phrase 'to Him be glory' is a direct expression of praise, and the term 'generations' emphasizes continuity through time.|
